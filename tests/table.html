This table shows the relationship between the "kind" attribute of findspots to the "population" attribute of toponyms. With this information, we can see the intersection between these two properties and whether or not they correlate, as well as the significance of this correlation.

In this case, "kind" (shown on the first row) refers to the type of material or medium in which the text was found at the archaeological site. An example of this is a clay tablet, or Tontafeln. On the other hand, "population" (shown in the first column of each row) refers to the people that inhabited the area, such as messengers or soldiers, for example.

To show more information about each population type, click on one of the rows. There you will see a list of all the toponyms that have that population attribute, as well as each kind attribute. Click the x in the top right corner to close this message. To show this message again, click 'Info' in the top left cell of the table.
